Welcome!

My name is Zdenek Prudil and I work as a ESO Fellow at the at the European Southern Observatory (ESO) in Garching by Munich, Germany. My research is focused on the Galactic archeology using the stellar tracers to map the spatial, dynamical, and chemical structure of the Milky Way and its neighborhood. I am also interested in studies of stellar evolution and pulsations.

Below you can find my latest research projects:

The Galactic bulge exploration: V. The secular spherical and X-shaped Milky Way bulge

In this fifth paper of the series, I focused on the full 6D spatial and kinematical distribution of RR Lyrae stars in the Milky Way’s central region. I discovered a dominant population of stars on retrograde orbits forming a nearly spherical, centrally concentrated structure. Their stable, non-chaotic motion suggests a long-lived, secular component of the bulge. These findings challenge classical interpretations and offer new insights into how internal Galactic dynamics can shape spheroidal stellar populations over time.

The Galactic bulge exploration: IV. RR Lyrae stars as tracers of the Galactic bar: 3D and 5D analysis and extinction variation

In this fourth paper of the series, I examined whether ancient RR Lyrae stars trace the Milky Way’s central bar. By creating new reddening maps and applying precise infrared distances, I showed that earlier signs of a bar in these stars were largely artifacts of dust extinction. However, a small metal-rich subgroup of RR Lyrae does align with the bar, suggesting a younger origin. This highlights how even the oldest stellar populations can reveal subtle layers of Galactic evolution.
